The size-controlled result is the real finding. After stratifying by vessel size, the efficiency effect is absent for small tankers (Δ −1.7pp) but substantial for medium and large vessels (Δ +8.7pp to +11.6pp). For ships large enough that ETS is a material cost, the dirtier ones flee at 9-12 percentage points higher rates. For small ships, efficiency does not matter — they leave regardless, likely driven by the fixed costs of MRV compliance relative to revenue. The carbon tax is selectively removing high-emitters from the segments that matter most for freight rates — the side effect is effective supply contraction concentrated in the larger vessel classes.

Methodology note: The tenure effect is not tanker-specific. Bulk carriers show the steepest gradient (51.5% dropout among new entrants), suggesting the least EU route commitment among recent arrivals. The consistency across three distinct vessel types strengthens the causal interpretation: ETS drives out the uncommitted, regardless of cargo type.

Even among established tankers — 5 consecutive years on EU routes — nearly 1 in 4 vanished when ETS hit. Among shorter-tenure vessels, it is 1 in 3. The gradient is the proof: the less committed the EU relationship, the higher the exit rate. Established tankers (23.1% dropout) had deep infrastructure ties, yet still left. Recent entrants (35.4% dropout) had no reason to stay. Critically, this pattern replicates across oil tankers, bulk carriers, and chemical tankers — it is not an artefact of one vessel class. This corrected analysis classifies vessels by their pre-2022 track record, eliminating the survivorship bias of counting total years active.

Oil Tanker "Effective Supply" Waterfall
Starting from global fleet, subtracting vessels removed from EU-accessible pool
The effective supply calculation for EU-touching tanker trades:
Start: ~11,000 oil tankers globally (Clarksons) → Remove 935 sanctioned (shadow fleet) → Remove ~425 that exited EU reporting (rerouted away) → Remove ~200 estimated slow-steaming for CII compliance → Remove rerouting absorption (Cape adds ~15% to voyage days) = Effective supply is ~25-30% below paper supply for EU routes. This is why VLCC spot hit $146K/day.
